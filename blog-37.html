You took a photo on your iPhone, tried to share it with a friend on Windows or upload it to a website, and it either didn't work or looked wrong. The culprit is almost always HEIC — the default photo format on iPhones since iOS 11. HEIC files are smaller than JPG at the same quality, which is great for iPhone storage, but they're not universally supported outside Apple's ecosystem.

The good news: converting HEIC to JPG is fast and free. This guide covers every method — on iPhone itself, on Mac, on Windows, and in the browser — so you can pick the one that fits your situation.

What Is HEIC and Why Is It a Problem?

HEIC (High Efficiency Image Container) is a file format developed by the MPEG group and adopted by Apple in 2017. It uses the HEVC codec to compress photos roughly twice as efficiently as JPG — meaning a HEIC photo takes up about half the storage of an equivalent-quality JPG. That's why Apple made it the default format for iPhone cameras.

The problem is compatibility. While macOS, iOS, and recent versions of Android support HEIC natively, Windows (without special plugins), most websites, most social media upload tools, and most photo editing software do not. If you've ever emailed a photo to someone on Windows and they saw a grey box, or tried to upload to a form that rejected your file — HEIC was likely the cause.

💡 HEIC vs HEIF

You may also see the extension .HEIF. These are essentially the same format — HEIF is the container format, and HEIC is the specific codec variant Apple uses. For conversion purposes, treat them identically.

Convert on iPhone (Before Sharing)

The easiest fix is to stop the problem at the source — configure your iPhone to capture in JPG instead of HEIC, or convert automatically when sharing.

Option 1: Change Camera Format to JPG

  1. Open Settings on your iPhone
  2. Tap Camera → Formats
  3. Select Most Compatible (this captures in JPG instead of HEIC)
⚠️ Trade-off

Shooting in JPG takes up roughly twice as much storage per photo. If storage space is tight on your iPhone, keep shooting in HEIC and use the automatic conversion method below when sharing instead.

Option 2: Auto-Convert When Sharing (Recommended)

Your iPhone can automatically convert HEIC to JPG whenever you share a photo to an app that doesn't support HEIC. Go to Settings → Camera → Formats and make sure "Transfer to Mac or PC" is set to "Automatic". When you AirDrop or cable-connect to a Windows PC, your iPhone will send JPG automatically.

For sharing via email or apps: when you tap Share on a photo and send it via iMessage, Mail, or most other apps, iOS automatically converts to JPG for recipients who don't support HEIC.

✅ Best for iPhone Users

This automatic conversion approach is the most seamless — you keep HEIC for storage efficiency but recipients always get compatible JPG files. No manual conversion needed.

Convert on Mac — Free Built-In Methods

Mac has excellent built-in HEIC support since macOS High Sierra, so conversion is straightforward with tools you already have.

Method 1: Preview (Quickest for Single Files)

  1. Open your HEIC file in Preview (double-click, or right-click → Open With → Preview)
  2. Go to File → Export
  3. Set Format to JPEG and adjust the Quality slider if desired
  4. Click Save

Method 2: Preview Batch Convert (Multiple Files at Once)

  1. Select all your HEIC files in Finder
  2. Right-click → Open With → Preview (this opens all files in one Preview window)
  3. In Preview, press ⌘+A to select all images in the sidebar
  4. Go to File → Export Selected Images
  5. Choose the output folder, set Format to JPEG, and click Choose

Method 3: Terminal (One Command for Entire Folders)

If you're comfortable with Terminal and have a folder full of HEIC files to convert, this command converts all of them at once:

Terminal Command
for f in *.HEIC; do sips -s format jpeg "$f" --out "${f%.HEIC}.jpg"; done

Run this in the Terminal after navigating to the folder containing your HEIC files. It converts every .HEIC file in that folder to .jpg at full quality.

Convert on Windows — Free Methods

Windows 10 and 11 don't natively support HEIC without a codec plugin, but there are several free ways to convert.

Method 1: Install the Free HEIF Image Extensions

  1. Open the Microsoft Store
  2. Search for "HEIF Image Extensions" — it's free from Microsoft
  3. Install it. Windows Photos, Paint, and most Windows apps will now open HEIC files natively
  4. To convert: open the HEIC in Photos, click the three-dot menu → Save as → choose JPEG
💡 Note

There's also a paid "HEVC Video Extensions" codec in the Store — you don't need that for photos, only for HEVC video. The free HEIF Image Extensions is all you need for HEIC photos.

Method 2: Use Paint or Paint 3D (After Installing HEIF Extensions)

Once the HEIF extension is installed, simply open your HEIC file in Paint, then go to File → Save As → JPEG picture. Quick and easy for single files.

Method 3: Online Converter (No Install Needed)

Does Converting HEIC to JPG Lose Quality?

Converting HEIC to JPG is a lossy process, but the quality loss is typically invisible at high quality settings. Here's what to know:

  • At quality 90–100%: The resulting JPG is visually indistinguishable from the HEIC original. This is the recommended setting for archiving or professional use.
  • At quality 75–85%: Slight compression artifacts may appear in very detailed areas. Fine for web use, social media, and email.
  • File size: A quality-85 JPG will be roughly 1.5–2x larger than the original HEIC. This is expected — JPG is less efficient than HEIC at equal quality.
  • Metadata: Most converters preserve EXIF metadata (date, camera settings, GPS location). Check your converter's documentation if metadata preservation is important.
✅ Recommended Quality Setting

For general use, convert at quality 85–90%. You'll get a file that looks identical to the original at a reasonable size. Only use 100% quality if you're archiving images for professional print use.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why are my iPhone photos saving as HEIC instead of JPG?
Apple set HEIC as the default camera format starting with iOS 11 because it saves about 50% storage vs JPG at equal quality. To switch back to JPG, go to Settings → Camera → Formats and select "Most Compatible."
Can I open HEIC files on Windows without converting them?
Yes — install the free "HEIF Image Extensions" from the Microsoft Store. After installation, Windows Photos, Paint, and most Windows apps can open HEIC files natively. No conversion needed just to view them.
Does converting HEIC to JPG delete the original HEIC file?
No. All the methods in this guide create a new JPG file alongside your original HEIC. Your original is not touched. You can delete the HEIC manually after you've confirmed the JPG looks correct.
Can I convert HEIC to PNG instead of JPG?
Yes. On Mac, use Preview → Export and choose PNG instead of JPEG. Online tools like Convertio also support HEIC to PNG. PNG is better if you need a transparent background or pixel-perfect quality, but JPG is fine for photos.
Are online HEIC converters safe to use?
Reputable services like iLoveIMG, Convertio, and CloudConvert are safe for non-sensitive photos. They delete uploaded files within minutes to hours. For sensitive images (personal IDs, private documents), use a local method like Mac Preview or Mac Terminal to avoid uploading to any server.
