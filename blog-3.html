When Should You Convert PDF to JPG?

  • Social media sharing: Platforms like Instagram and Twitter don't support PDFs — convert to JPG first
  • Presentations: Insert PDF pages as images in PowerPoint or Google Slides
  • Previews: Create thumbnail previews of PDF documents
  • Editing: Edit PDF content in image editing apps
  • Uploading to forms: Many online forms only accept image files
  • Quick sharing: JPG files are universally supported on all devices

Understanding Quality Settings

  • High quality (90-100%): Best for printing, archiving, and professional presentations. Large file size.
  • Medium quality (75-85%): Perfect for digital sharing, websites and presentations. Recommended.
  • Lower quality (50-70%): Good for quick previews and thumbnails. Smallest file size.

❓ Does it convert all pages automatically?
Yes! All pages in your PDF are converted to individual JPG images automatically. You can then download each page separately.
❓ What happens to text in the PDF?
Text is rendered as part of the image, exactly as it appears in the PDF. The text is no longer editable but looks exactly the same visually.
❓ Can I convert a scanned PDF?
Absolutely! Scanned PDFs convert perfectly to JPG images since they are already image-based content.
