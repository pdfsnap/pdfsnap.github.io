How to Add a Watermark to PDF Files to Protect Your Work

📅 March 20, 2026 ⏱️ 5 min read 👤 PDFSnap Team

Watermarking your documents and images is one of the most effective ways to protect your work, establish ownership, and prevent unauthorized use. Whether you're a photographer, business owner, or just want to mark confidential documents — this guide shows you exactly how to do it for free.

Why Add Watermarks to Your PDF Documents?

  • Prove ownership: Clearly mark documents as yours with your name or company
  • Protect confidential info: Mark sensitive documents as CONFIDENTIAL or DRAFT
  • Prevent unauthorized copying: Visible watermarks discourage plagiarism
  • Professional branding: Add your business name or website to all documents
  • Document status: Mark documents as APPROVED, REJECTED, or SAMPLE
  • Legal protection: Watermarks serve as evidence of ownership in disputes

Types of Watermarks

📝
Text Watermark
Words like CONFIDENTIAL, DRAFT, your name or company stamped across the page.
🔒
Security Watermark
CLASSIFIED, FOR REVIEW ONLY, DO NOT COPY — protects sensitive documents.

💡 Pro Tip: Font Size Matters

Use a font size between 36-60 for a visible but not overwhelming watermark. Very small watermarks are easy to crop out. Very large ones obscure the content too much.

Best Watermark Text Ideas

  • For confidential documents: CONFIDENTIAL · CLASSIFIED · PRIVATE · SENSITIVE
  • For drafts: DRAFT · FOR REVIEW · NOT FINAL · SAMPLE
  • For ownership: Your Name · Your Company Name · © 2026 YourBrand
  • For copies: COPY · DUPLICATE · FOR REFERENCE ONLY
  • For approval status: APPROVED · REJECTED · PENDING REVIEW
