What Is WebP and Why Does It Cause Problems

WebP is an image format developed by Google in 2010. It produces images approximately 25 to 34 percent smaller than JPG at equivalent quality — which is why virtually every major website now serves images in WebP to improve page loading speed.

The problem is compatibility. While all modern browsers display WebP perfectly, many other applications do not support it:

  • Older photo editing software: Photoshop before 2021, many free editors, and older versions of Paint do not open WebP.
  • Upload portals and forms: Most only accept JPG, PNG, and sometimes GIF — not WebP.
  • Printing services: Virtually no print service accepts WebP files.
  • WhatsApp and messaging apps: May show WebP as a download rather than displaying it as an image.
📌 Why You Downloaded WebP Instead of JPG

When you right-click and Save Image from most websites in 2026, you get WebP because that is what the website is serving for browser performance. Converting the downloaded WebP back to JPG is the standard solution.

Does Converting WebP to JPG Lose Quality?

Technically yes, but practically invisible at high quality settings. WebP uses more efficient compression than JPG, so converting to JPG at equivalent visual quality results in a slightly larger file. At JPG quality 90 percent or higher, the visual difference from the original WebP is imperceptible at normal viewing sizes.

⚠️ Do Not Convert Back to WebP After Converting to JPG

Converting WebP to JPG to WebP applies compression twice. Each round of lossy compression degrades quality slightly. Always work from the original WebP if you need a WebP output.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do my downloaded images keep saving as WebP?
Websites serve WebP by default to all modern browsers because it loads faster. When you right-click and save you get the WebP version. Convert it to JPG using any of the methods above.
Can I convert WebP to PNG instead of JPG?
Yes — if your WebP has a transparent background, convert to PNG to preserve transparency. JPG does not support transparency and fills transparent areas with white.
Does Windows 11 support WebP natively?
Yes — Windows 11 supports WebP in Photos app, Paint, and File Explorer thumbnails. However many third-party applications still do not support WebP, so conversion is often still needed.
