Why Convert PDF to JPG on Mobile

  • WhatsApp and Telegram sharing: Images display inline in chat. A JPG appears immediately in the conversation rather than requiring the recipient to open a separate PDF viewer.
  • Social media: Instagram, Facebook, and Twitter only accept image formats. Converting a PDF certificate or infographic to JPG lets you share it directly.
  • Portal uploads that require images: HR systems, university portals, and government forms often specify JPG or PNG only — PDFs are not accepted.
  • Saving certificates to gallery: Saving your degree or completion certificate as a photo makes it instantly accessible in your phone gallery for sharing any time.
  • Non-tech recipients: Many people struggle to open PDF attachments on phones. A JPG opens instantly in any gallery app.

iPhone Built-in Options

Screenshot Method

Open the PDF in Files or Safari, navigate to the page you want, and take a screenshot (Side button + Volume Up). Screenshots capture at screen resolution — good for sharing, not suitable for printing. The quality limitation is significant: a screenshot is limited to your display resolution rather than the document's native resolution.

Markup in Files App

Some iOS versions allow saving individual PDF pages as images through the Files app. Open the PDF → long-press on a page → look for a Save as Image option. Availability varies by iOS version.

Android Built-in Options

Screenshot Method

Open the PDF in any viewer, navigate to your target page, and take a screenshot (Volume Down + Power simultaneously on most Android phones). Quality is limited to screen resolution.

Google Drive

Upload the PDF to Google Drive → open it → tap 3 dots → Open with Google Docs. In Docs, images can sometimes be saved individually via long-press. Reliability varies for complex PDFs.

📌 Best Android Method

For reliable, high-quality PDF to JPG conversion on Android, the browser-based PDFSnap method is always the best choice. It works on every Android phone with Chrome installed.

Tips for Best Mobile Conversion Quality

  • Use the original PDF file, not a screenshot of it. Screenshots lose quality immediately.
  • Choose High quality when the JPG will be used professionally or printed.
  • Close other browser tabs first if your phone is slow during conversion — this frees up memory for processing.
  • Check file size after conversion. A good quality single-page JPG should be 200KB to 2MB depending on content.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does PDFSnap work on iPhone Safari?
Yes — PDFSnap works in Safari, Chrome, Firefox, and any other browser on iPhone. The tool runs using JavaScript inside your browser tab, so it works on iOS the same as desktop. No app install needed.
Where do converted JPG files save on iPhone?
Downloaded files go to your Files app Downloads folder by default in Safari. In Chrome they also go to Files. You can then move them to Photos using Share button → Save Image, or share directly from Files.
Can I convert a multi-page PDF to multiple JPGs on mobile?
Yes. PDFSnap converts each page to a separate JPG. For a 10-page PDF you get 10 JPG files that download individually.
What is the difference between a screenshot and proper PDF to JPG conversion?
A screenshot captures only what is visible on screen at screen resolution. A proper conversion renders the PDF at full document resolution — giving you a higher quality image suitable for printing, professional sharing, and portal uploads.
